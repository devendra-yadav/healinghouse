Welcome!

This guide will help you use the Healing House Clinic system like a pro. Think of this system as your digital notebook — it keeps track of patients, appointments, payments, and everything else, so you don't have to remember it all yourself.

You don't need any computer knowledge to use it. Everything is done by clicking buttons and filling simple forms, just like filling a paper form — except this one saves itself and never gets lost!

Good to know: There is no login needed right now — anyone on the clinic computer can open the system and start working straightaway.

The Dashboard

This is the very first screen you see. It gives you a quick snapshot of how the clinic is doing today.

What you'll see here
  • Today's Appointments — how many visits are booked for today.
  • Today's Revenue — how much money has come in today.
  • Low Stock Items — products that are running out and need reordering.
  • Active Therapists — how many therapists are currently working with you.
  • A list of Today's Appointments, so you can see who's coming in and when.
  • A Low Stock Alerts box that tells you exactly which products need restocking.
  • Two simple charts — revenue over the last 7 days, and which tags (categories) earned the most over the last 30 days.

Quick Actions

Right below the summary cards, you'll find shortcut buttons — New Appointment, New Patient, and View Reports — so you don't have to hunt through menus for the things you do most often.

Patients

This is where every patient's record lives — their contact details, medical notes, visit history, and payments.

Adding a new patient
  1. Click Patients in the top menu.
  2. Click the Add Patient button.
  3. Fill in the patient's Full Name and Phone number — these are the only required fields.
  4. If you have them, add Email, Gender, and Date of Birth (this lets the system calculate their age automatically).
  5. Add Address, Medical History, and any known Allergies — optional, but very useful for the therapist to know before treatment.
  6. Add any general Notes if needed.
  7. Click Save Patient. That's it — the patient is now in the system!
[Screenshot: New Patient form with Full Name, Phone, Email, Gender, DOB, Address, Medical History, Allergies]
Always write down allergies. If a patient has any allergy — to oils, creams, or anything else — please write it in the Allergies box. It shows up in red on their profile so nobody misses it.
Finding a patient

On the Patients page, just start typing the patient's name or phone number in the search box. Matching names pop up as you type — no need to press any button.

The Patient Profile page

Click on any patient's name (or the "view" icon) to open their full profile. Here you'll find:

Patient Profile Contact details, medical history, and allergies, all in one place.
Summary cards Total visits, lifetime money spent, last visit date, favourite therapist, top service/product.
Prepaid Balance (Wallet) Advance money the patient has kept with the clinic — see Wallet.
Packages Session bundles the patient has bought, and how many are left — see Packages.

There's also a full Appointment History table with filters for therapist, status, and date range.

Editing a patient

Open the patient's profile and click Edit Patient at the top. Make your changes and click Save Patient again.

Therapists

Keep track of your therapists, their specialities, and how they are paid.

Adding a therapist
  1. Click Therapists in the top menu, then Add Therapist.
  2. Enter their Name, Phone, Email, and Specialization (e.g. "Deep Tissue, Ayurveda").
  3. If this therapist gets a fixed pay, enter their Monthly Salary.
  4. If they earn a percentage of what they bring in, enter their Commission Rate.
  5. If they get an extra bonus for hitting a target, fill in the Bonus Threshold (number of services) and Bonus Amount.
  6. If this therapist is the clinic owner, tick "This therapist is the owner" — owners don't get commission/bonus calculated.
  7. Click Save.
The Therapist Profile page

Open any therapist's profile to see their details plus an Earnings Summary for a date range you choose (defaults to the current month): appointments handled, total revenue, Total Commission, any Bonus earned, and Total Variable Pay.

Commission only counts tagged items. A service or product only counts towards a therapist's commission if it's tagged "Commission", and only counts towards their bonus target if tagged "Bonus". See Tags below — this is important, otherwise a therapist's pay may come out lower than expected!
Therapist Calendar

Click the Calendar button on a therapist's profile to see a day/week/month view of all their appointments — handy for checking who's free before booking a new visit.

Services & Products

Services are the treatments you offer (like "Deep Tissue Massage" or "Facial"). Products are physical items you sell (like oils or creams) and have a stock count.

Adding a service or product
  1. Click Services or Products in the top menu.
  2. Click Add Service / Add Product.
  3. Enter the Name, Price, and a short Description if you like.
  4. For products, also enter the current Stock Quantity and the Reorder Level (when the Dashboard should warn you it's running low).
  5. Add one or more Tags to help organise and report on it.
  6. Click Save.
Stock goes down automatically. When an appointment using a product is marked Completed, the stock count reduces by itself. You never need to update stock by hand for a sale.
Removing something from the catalogue

You'll almost never need to permanently delete a service or product. Instead, click the trash icon to Deactivate it — this hides it from new bookings but keeps every past appointment record exactly as it was. Bring it back any time using the Show Inactive toggle and the restore button.

Only if a service/product was never used in any appointment and is already deactivated will you see an option to permanently delete it — this removes it forever, so use it carefully.

Tags

Tags are simple labels you attach to services and products, like "Facial," "Ayurveda," "Commission," or "Bonus." They help you group things for reports and for calculating therapist pay.

  1. Click Tags in the top menu to see all existing tags.
  2. You can rename, merge two similar tags into one, or delete a tag you no longer need.
  3. To add a tag to a service or product, open its Edit page and type into the Tags box — it will suggest existing tags as you type, or let you create a new one.
Two tags matter for therapist pay. Tag a service or product "Commission" if it should count towards a therapist's commission. Tag a service "Bonus" if it should count towards their monthly bonus target. Miss the tag, and it silently earns the therapist ₹0 towards pay — even though the sale itself is recorded correctly.
Combos

A Combo is a bundle of services and/or products sold together at a discount — for example, "Massage + Facial Combo" for a special price instead of paying for each separately.

Creating a combo
  1. Click Combos in the top menu, then New Combo.
  2. Give it a Name and, if you like, a short Description.
  3. Add the services and/or products that make up the combo, with the quantity of each.
  4. Choose how the combo is discounted — a Percentage off, or a Flat ₹ amount off.
  5. Click Save.

You never need to type the combo's price by hand — the system always works it out live from the current prices of the items inside it, so it's always accurate even if you change a service's price later.

Selling a combo during booking

When creating an appointment, click Choose Combo (next to "Add Service") to open the combo picker, search for the combo, and add it. All its items are added together as a locked group, with a savings badge shown on the appointment.

Combos are all-or-nothing. Once a combo is added to an appointment, you can't remove just one item from it — only the whole combo via "Remove Combo." You can still change which therapist did each item though.
Packages

A Package is when a patient pays upfront for several sessions to use over multiple future visits — like "10 Back Massages" bought all at once. This is different from a Combo (used up in a single visit) and different from Wallet money (a general ₹ balance, not tied to a specific service).

Setting up a Package Template (optional shortcut)

A template just makes selling common packages faster — it's completely optional.

  1. Click Packages in the top menu, then New Template.
  2. Give it a Name (e.g. "10x Back Massage").
  3. Add the service(s)/product(s) included, and how many sessions of each.
  4. Click Save. The suggested price is calculated automatically from current prices — you can still adjust it when actually selling.
Selling a package to a patient
  1. Open the patient's profile page.
  2. In the Packages card, click Sell Package.
  3. Pick a template to start from (or choose "— Custom package —" to build one from scratch).
  4. Check or adjust the sessions and the Total Price you're charging.
  5. Click Sell.
[Screenshot: Sell Package modal on the patient page, with template picker and item rows]
Using up package sessions during a booking

When you book an appointment for that patient, look for Already Paid on the booking form. It automatically shows any prepaid sessions the patient still has remaining. Just click to add — no extra payment needed for that item, since it's already been paid for through the package.

Refunding an unused package

On the patient's profile, click Refund next to the package. The system automatically works out the fair refund value based on how many sessions are left unused.

Packages can expire. If you set an expiry date when selling, an unused package automatically shows as "Expired" once that date passes. It can still be refunded — nothing is ever silently lost, but staff must action a refund manually.
Booking an Appointment

This is what you'll do most often — booking a visit for a patient with a therapist.

  1. Click Appointments in the top menu, then New Appointment (or use the shortcut on the Dashboard).
  2. Choose the Patient and Therapist from the dropdowns. You can type to search.
  3. Pick the Date & Time and how long the visit will take (Duration).
  4. Click Add Service to add each treatment being done. Pick the service, how many, and (if different from the main therapist) who's actually doing it.
  5. If any products are being used or sold, click Add Product the same way.
  6. If this booking should use a combo, click Choose Combo instead — see Combos.
  7. If the patient has prepaid package sessions available, they'll appear under Already Paid — click to apply them.
  8. Enter a Discount if you're giving one (percentage or flat ₹) — it splits automatically and fairly across every item.
  9. Choose the Payment Method and type how much the patient is paying right now under Amount Paid.
  10. If the patient wants to use their Prepaid Balance (Wallet), enter that amount too — see Wallet.
  11. Check the Summary box on the right — it shows the Grand Total and Balance Due, updating live as you type.
  12. Click Save Appointment.
[Screenshot: New Appointment form — Patient/Therapist selectors, Services table, Payment section, running Summary box]
If two appointments clash

If the therapist you picked is already busy at that time, the system warns you with an orange banner — but it will not stop you from saving. If the double-booking is intentional (or acceptable), tick "Save anyway" and save again.

Nothing you typed gets lost. If a scheduling clash pops up, all your entries stay exactly as you typed them — you don't need to fill the form again.
About the amount paid

The system will never let the amount paid go above the Grand Total — if you type too much, you'll see a red warning telling you to fix it before saving.

Managing an Appointment

Open any appointment (from the Appointments list, Dashboard, or a patient's history) to see its full details and take action.

What you can do while it's still "Scheduled"
Edit Change services, products, time, discount, or payment before the visit happens.
Complete Mark the visit as finished once treatment is done. Clear any balance due first.
No-Show Mark it if the patient simply didn't turn up.
Cancel Cancel and type a short reason — shown on the appointment afterwards.
Cancelling or No-Show reverses money automatically. If the patient had used Wallet or Package sessions for this visit, cancelling or marking No-Show automatically gives those back to the patient — you don't need to fix it by hand.
Status colours, at a glance
SCHEDULEDBooked, upcoming or in progress. Can still be edited.
COMPLETEDVisit finished. Locked from further edits.
NO_SHOWPatient did not come in.
CANCELLEDAppointment was called off.
PaidFully paid — nothing owing.
PartialSome money is still due.
UnpaidNo payment made yet.
Reassigning who did a service or product

If a different therapist actually performed one item (say, they covered for a colleague), open the appointment and change the therapist on that specific line — this keeps commission reporting accurate for whoever really did the work.

Prepaid Balance (Wallet)

Think of this as money the patient keeps "on account" with the clinic — like a gift card balance — that they can use towards any future visit, for any service.

Topping up a patient's balance
  1. Open the patient's profile, or open any of their appointments.
  2. Click Top Up.
  3. Enter the amount and how they paid (Cash, UPI, Card, etc.).
  4. Click Confirm. The balance updates instantly.
Using the balance during a booking

On the appointment form, type the amount to draw from the wallet in the Apply from Wallet box. It shows the patient's available balance right there, and warns you in red if you try to use more than they have, or more than the bill actually needs.

Refunding balance

On the patient's profile, click Refund next to the Prepaid Balance card, enter the amount, and confirm.

Every rupee is tracked. Every top-up, use, refund, or reversal is recorded in a running history table on the patient's page, so you can always see exactly where the money went.
While editing an appointment: the wallet box on an existing appointment shows how much is already applied. Type only the extra amount you want to add now — don't retype the full total, or you may accidentally overwrite what was already recorded. Use the small pencil icon only if you need to correct a mistaken entry.
Reports

Click Reports in the top menu to see six report types. Each can be viewed on-screen, and downloaded as a CSV (for Excel) or a nicely formatted PDF, using the export buttons at the top of the report.

ReportWhat it tells you
Daily ReportRevenue and each therapist's earnings for one specific day.
Period ReportEarnings, tag revenue, and product performance over a date range you choose.
Therapist ComparisonCompare two or more therapists side by side.
Patient AcquisitionNew vs. repeat patients, and how well you retain them.

Actual RevenueThe real money billed and collected, after discounts — the truest picture of clinic income.
[Screenshot: Reports home page showing all six report cards]
"Revenue" on most reports is before discounts. Five of the six reports show figures labelled "(Pre-Discount)" — intentional, because commission is always calculated on the full price, even if you gave the patient a discount. If you want the actual money collected after every discount, always check the Actual Revenue report instead.
Downloading a report
  1. Open the report you need and set your filters (date range, therapist, etc.).
  2. Click Export CSV for a spreadsheet, or Export PDF for a printable, letterhead-branded document.
  3. The file downloads straight to your computer — open it from your Downloads folder.
Tips & Common Questions
"I made a mistake — can I fix it?"

Yes! As long as the appointment is still Scheduled, click Edit and correct anything — patient, therapist, items, discount, or payment. Once it's marked Completed, it's locked to keep the financial record accurate, so double-check before completing.

"Can I delete a patient, service, or product by mistake?"

Not easily — the system protects you here. You can only Deactivate things by default, which safely hides them without touching any history. A true permanent delete is only allowed for items that have never been used anywhere, so old records are always safe.

"Where do I see how much a therapist should be paid?"

Open their Therapist Profile page and check the Earnings Summary — remember, only items tagged "Commission" and "Bonus" count towards their pay.

"What's the difference between a Combo, a Package, and the Wallet?"
FeatureHow it works
ComboA bundle used up entirely in one single visit, at a bundled price.
PackageA set number of sessions bought in advance, used one at a time over several future visits.
WalletA general ₹ balance the patient keeps on account, usable towards anything, any time.
"The page looks a little different on my phone — is that normal?"

Yes, completely normal! Every page automatically rearranges itself to fit smaller screens, so it stays easy to use on a tablet or phone at the front desk.

Still stuck? If something looks unfamiliar or a page shows an error, just note down what you were doing and ask your manager or the person who set up the system for help.
